Prism

Real-time Misinformation Defense

Our mission is to slow the spread of misinformation and restore critical thinking online.

Prism is a cross-browser extension that flags questionable claims, highlights bias and framing signals, and provides citations you can trust — all without pulling you out of your workflow.

Our Mission

Truth should be easier to find than manipulation.

We’re building Prism to reduce misinformation at the point of consumption. Instead of hoping people “look it up later,” Prism helps them verify claims immediately, see missing context, and think more critically before they share.

We believe the internet is drifting toward information overload, performative certainty, and engineered outrage. Prism is a tool that nudges the web back toward evidence, nuance, and honest uncertainty — with receipts.

Claim-level Verification

Identify verifiable claims and cross-check against independent sources

Citations, Not Vibes

Show evidence and counter-evidence with clear source trails

Framing + Bias Signals

Detect loaded language, missing context, and one-sided framing

Prism runs a fast “quick scan” on-page, then deepens verification only when it matters.
How It Works

A real-time layer of clarity on top of the internet.

Prism follows a simple philosophy: detect claims, verify with evidence, and show uncertainty honestly. It’s designed to stay fast and non-disruptive, so you can keep reading while Prism does the heavy lifting.

1) Extract verifiable claims

Find statements that can be checked (numbers, dates, causal claims, “X happened,” etc.)

2) Quick scan on-page

Instant risk signals: weak sourcing, suspicious certainty, pattern matches, and inconsistencies

3) Retrieve + cross-check

Pull multiple independent sources, summarize agreement, and provide citations + confidence

4) Context + framing

Highlight bias signals and missing context so you can judge the claim in full
